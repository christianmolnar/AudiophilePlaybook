Kenwood KD-500 Turntable

Vintage Direct Drive Classic
The Kenwood KD-500, often nicknamed 'The Rock', is a classic direct-drive turntable from the late 1970s. Renowned for its stability and performance, this page serves as a reference for setup, maintenance, and potential upgrades for this vintage gem.

Motor20-pole DC Servo

My Kenwood KD-500 Turntable

My personal Kenwood KD-500, is a cornerstone of my vinyl setup. I acquired it from a very kind gentleman right here in the Seattle area. It came equipped with the highly regarded, low-mass ADC LMF-2 tonearm, a perfect match for high-compliance cartridges. Currently, it sings with a Stanton 681EEE-S stylus, known for its warm, smooth, and detailed sound signature â€“ just excellent for any music genre.

What makes this particular KD-500 unique is its stunning finish. The previous owner had a background in automotive paint and body work, and he applied his skills beautifully, giving it a deep, glossy black lacquer finish that looks absolutely factory-perfect. It's a testament to both Kenwood's original engineering and the care of its previous owner.
